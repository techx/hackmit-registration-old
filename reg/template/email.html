The HackMIT 2014 Lottery Registration is Open!

HackMIT is back this Fall on October 4-5.

Last year, over 1000 students from around the world came to MIT and created some truly awesome hacks. This fall, HackMIT is back! Come meet a ton of awesome people and build software and hardware projects over the course of 24 hours.

This year, registration is a bit different.

We're taking a page from Google I/O's book and doing completely random admission. Over the next fifteen days, you can enter the lottery for admission to HackMIT, and at the end, we'll pick 1000 people to let in. However, if you arent chosen, don't despair--as people make the final decision on whether or not to attend, we will release more spots to more hackers.

Who can register?

All undergraduate students from any university are welcome. Non-MIT students must be 18 or older, and unfortunately high school and graduate students cannot attend.

About team formation.

If you are admitted to HackMIT, you will be given an access code that you can share with up to three teammates; these teammates must join your team. So, don't worry if only some members of your team get in--if one member is admitted, the rest can come.

A Puzzling Loophole.

HackMIT is taking another tip from Google I/O--we've set up a HackMIT admissions puzzle. The start to this puzzle is hidden on the HackMIT website. If you complete this puzzle, you will be automatically admitted to HackMIT. We'd like to challenge everyone reading this email to reach the end.

For MIT hackers:

If you will be an undergraduate at MIT this fall, don't worry--you'll automatically get let in. However, please still sign up for the lottery, as you'll need an account for team formation, day-of check-in, etc.

Have more questions?

A list of FAQ will soon be posted on our website and will include details about travel reimbursement, hardware supplies, and more. We will also email out with more registration details after the lottery closes. May the odds be ever in your favor!
